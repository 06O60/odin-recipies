Tigre Chocolate Financier Recipe

Description

it is a baked pastry called Tigre, a moist financier dough with burnt butter and an irresistible Anglaise base ganache. In French, "tigre" means tiger, and the pattern of this pastry comes from the fact that it resembles a tiger.

Ingredients

  • Parter Financier
    • Unsalted butter: 110g
    • Egg white: 100g
    • Sugar syrup (invert sugar): 15g
    • Granulated sugar: 75g
    • Almond powder: 120g
    • Flour: 25g
    • Chocolate chips for baking: 50g
  • Ganache
    • Egg yolk: 25g
    • Granulated sugar: 25g
    • Milk: 125g
    • Granulated sugar: 10g
    • Chocolate 65%: 100g

Steps

  • Parter Financier
    1. Heat the butter in a pan to make browned butter.
    2. When the butter is brown and foamy, transfer it to a bowl and let it cool.
    3. Put the egg whites in a bowl and cut them into small pieces.
    4. Add the syrup and mix, then add the granulated sugar.
    5. Sift in the almond powder and mix thoroughly.
    6. Cool the browned butter to around 30째C and mix.
    7. Add in the flour and mix until no more flour is needed.
    8. Add the chocolate chips and mix quickly with a rubber spatula.
    9. Chill in the refrigerator for about 3 hours.
    10. Pour the dough into a Savarin mold and make a dent in the center.
    11. Preheat the oven to 180째C and bake for 14-15 minutes.
    12. Bake for 14-15 minutes at 180째C. Cool on a rack.
    13. Keep sealed in the refrigerator for one day to moisten.
  • Ganache
    1. Chop the chocolate and set aside.
    2. Blanch the egg yolks with the granulated sugar.
    3. Bring the milk to a boil and add half of it to the egg yolks.
    4. Return the egg yolk mixture to the pan and heat to 82째C to thicken.
    5. Add the egg yolk mixture to the chopped chocolate and mix with a hand blender.
    6. Place in a squeeze bag and squeeze or store in the refrigerator.
